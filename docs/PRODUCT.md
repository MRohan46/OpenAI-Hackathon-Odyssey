# Odyssey

> **From Zero to Hero, One Quest at a Time.**

Most habit trackers are very good at asking one question:

> Did you do it?

That answer is useful, but it captures only one part of the work.

Doing ten minutes of distracted study and completing a focused ninety-minute session are not the same achievement. Checking off an easy task and finishing the one activity that actually moves your life forward are not the same victory. Maintaining a habit for three weeks says more than tapping the same checkbox twice and disappearing.

That difference is where Odyssey begins.

**Odyssey is a gamified calendar, habit tracker, and long-term goal system that measures consistency, intensity, priority, and real progress.** It turns daily habits into quests, meaningful milestones into mini-bosses, and the goal waiting at the end of the journey into a final boss worth defeating.

Odyssey is a productivity system with a game at its heart, built around a simple belief:

> A goal becomes achievable when the path toward it is visible, the next step is clear, and showing up feels rewarding.

The checkmark matters. The journey behind it matters more.

---

## The Problem

People rarely abandon a goal because they never cared about it.

They abandon it because the distance between **where they are** and **where they want to be** becomes difficult to understand. A large goal lives somewhere in the future, today's effort feels small, and a traditional task list offers no convincing answer to the question:

> Is any of this actually getting me closer?

Most calendars and habit trackers make this worse in four ways.

### They measure frequency without measuring effort

A habit receives the same checkmark whether the session was light, normal, or intense. The user completed something, but the tracker learned almost nothing about the quality of that work.

### They treat every task as equally important

Organizing a desk can sit beside studying for an examination as if both activities contribute equally. The easiest task wins attention because it is easy to complete, while the task that matters stays buried in the list.

### They separate daily work from long-term ambition

The calendar knows what happens today. The goal-setting page knows what should happen someday. Very few products connect those two worlds in a way the user can feel.

### They record failure without creating recovery

Miss one day, lose a streak, and returning can suddenly feel harder than missing the habit in the first place. Accountability should help users face the miss and take the next step, not give them another reason to quit.

Odyssey is built around a different approach.

---

## The Odyssey Answer

Odyssey turns progress into a journey with a beginning, a path, meaningful challenges, and a visible finish line.

A user begins with an ambition:

- Prepare for an important examination
- Build a consistent fitness routine
- Learn a new language
- Finish a portfolio
- Develop a creative skill
- Complete a personal project

Odyssey uses AI to transform that ambition into an editable ten-level roadmap. The user reviews the plan, changes anything that does not fit their life, and activates it only when it feels right.

From there, the plan stops being an intimidating paragraph and becomes something playable:

- Levels become stages of progress.
- Habits become repeatable quests.
- One-time tasks become missions.
- Important milestones become mini-bosses.
- The final goal becomes the final boss.
- Consistency builds streaks.
- Meaningful effort earns XP and rubies.
- Major victories unlock chests, boosts, and cosmetic rewards.

The fantasy is fun. The structure underneath it is serious.

Odyssey does not promise that a difficult goal will become effortless. It makes the effort understandable, manageable, and much harder to ignore.

## The Core Journey

Every Odyssey follows one clear loop.

### 1. Choose the destination

The user creates a long-term goal and describes what success means. A useful goal can include a deadline, current ability, available days, time available per day, and any real-life constraints.

### 2. Build the roadmap

Groq AI turns the goal into ten structured levels with suggested habits and one-time tasks. The generated plan is a starting point, never the final word. The user can edit, remove, reorder, or regenerate any part before accepting it.

### 3. Schedule the work

Accepted habits and tasks enter Odyssey's own calendar. The user chooses recurrence, scheduled times, deadlines, priorities, intended intensity, and whether photo proof is required.

### 4. Enter the Daily Quest Board

Each day, Odyssey gathers the work that matters now. Critical quests rise above low-value distractions, deadlines remain visible, and the next meaningful action stays clear even on a busy day.

### 5. Complete the quest

The user records the session, confirms its actual intensity, and attaches photo proof when that habit requires it. Completion contributes to streaks, analytics, rewards, roadmap progress, and boss health.

### 6. Earn the victory

Completed quests grant XP and rubies. Milestones unlock chests and boosts. Connected quests damage the active boss. Every part of that progress comes from work the user actually completed.

### 7. Reach the next shore

The roadmap advances. Mini-bosses fall at levels 3, 6, and 8. Level 10 holds the final boss: the completed version of the goal the user once thought was too large.

Then the user begins another Odyssey.

Because one conquered goal is not the ending. It is proof.

## Odyssey Calendar

Odyssey includes its own calendar designed around habits, quests, and long-term progress.

It gives users a familiar way to answer practical questions:

- What must I do today?
- What is scheduled this week?
- Which deadline is approaching?
- Which habits are recurring?
- What did I complete?
- What did I miss?
- Which goal does this work support?

The calendar is not dependent on Google Calendar and does not require external synchronization. It belongs to Odyssey, which means every scheduled activity can understand priority, intensity, proof requirements, rewards, streaks, roadmap connections, and boss progress.

That context is what turns a block of time into a quest.

### Habits and tasks

Odyssey supports two kinds of work without making either one complicated:

- **Habits** are repeatable actions performed according to a schedule.
- **Tasks** are one-time actions completed once.

They share the same quest system, so users receive consistent controls and rewards while still understanding whether an activity repeats.

Users can create, view, edit, reschedule, complete, or remove both. A quest can include:

- A title and useful description
- A scheduled date and time
- A deadline
- Low, Medium, High, or Critical priority
- Light, Normal, or Intense effort
- A recurring schedule when it is a habit
- A connection to one of the user's active Odysseys
- A configurable photo-proof requirement

The goal is not to turn creating a habit into filing taxes. Odyssey asks for enough information to make the quest meaningful, then gets out of the way.

### Recurring schedules

Habits can repeat:

- Every day
- Every week
- On selected days
- At a custom interval

Recurring habits automatically return to the calendar and Daily Quest Board according to their schedule. Each occurrence keeps its own completion state, recorded intensity, proof, and reward history.

The habit stays familiar. The evidence of consistency grows around it.

## Daily Quest Board

The Daily Quest Board is where the day's work comes together.

It gathers scheduled habits, one-time tasks, approaching deadlines, overdue work, active roadmap objectives, and boss-related quests into one focused view. Instead of forcing the user to inspect an entire calendar, it answers one question immediately:

> What deserves my attention right now?

Priority decides what rises to the top.

### Priority levels

Every quest can be assigned one of four priorities:

| Priority | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Low** | Useful work that can wait when more important commitments demand attention |
| **Medium** | Meaningful work that should be completed as planned |
| **High** | Important work that directly advances a major goal |
| **Critical** | Time-sensitive or essential work that cannot be casually ignored |

High and Critical quests receive stronger attention and can carry greater rewards because completing meaningful work should matter more than farming easy checkmarks.

Priority and deadline remain separate:

- Priority answers **how important is this?**
- Deadline answers **when must this be finished?**

A Critical task due next week and a Medium task due tonight are different situations. Odyssey respects both.

---

## Deadlines and Reminders

A quest can have a scheduled starting time, a deadline, or both.

Odyssey supports device notifications and in-app reminders so important work does not disappear the moment the application closes.

Reminders can appear:

- Before a scheduled quest begins
- As a deadline approaches
- When a quest becomes overdue

The language belongs to Odyssey's world without hiding the actual information:

> Your training quest begins in 15 minutes.

> One hour remains before today's quest expires.

> The Study Boss is still standing. Your next strike is due before 8:00 PM.

The user should always understand what needs to happen and when. Theme adds energy; clarity keeps the system useful.

---

## Intensity Tracking

Completion alone cannot describe effort.

Odyssey records session intensity at three levels:

- **Light** — The user showed up and made a smaller effort.
- **Normal** — The user completed the intended session at its expected effort.
- **Intense** — The user performed a demanding, deeply focused session.

A habit can carry a planned intensity, while the user records the actual intensity after completing it. This allows Odyssey to compare intention with reality.

That distinction matters.

If a user plans five intense sessions and repeatedly manages only light ones, the answer is not shame. The answer may be a plan that does not fit their life. If a user consistently exceeds the intended effort, that deserves to appear in their progress.

Odyssey therefore measures more than whether the user appeared. It helps them understand how they appeared.

---

## Consistency and Streaks

Consistency is the bridge between a good intention and a changed life.

Odyssey tracks streaks at two levels:

- **Habit streaks** show how consistently each recurring habit is completed.
- **The overall Odyssey streak** shows how consistently the user completes the day's required quests.

This gives users an honest picture. Someone may have a powerful reading streak while struggling with exercise, or maintain a strong overall routine while rebuilding one difficult habit. A single number should not erase that story.

### Missing a quest

Odyssey has consequences, but it does not destroy earned progress.

When a required quest is missed:

- Its available completion rewards are lost.
- The relevant habit or daily streak may break.
- The missed occurrence remains visible in progress history.
- Previously earned XP is never removed.
- Completed roadmap progress is never erased.
- Bosses do not recover health already defeated through real work.

The user can fail a day without becoming a failure.

Odyssey keeps the stakes, records the truth, and makes the next useful action visible. That is accountability designed to help the user continue.

---

## Photo Proof

Some habits benefit from evidence. Others do not.

A completed workout can be supported by a photograph. A finished art session can show the work created. A cleaned space can be captured directly. Meditation, reflection, or private health routines may not need or deserve a camera.

For that reason, photo proof is configured per habit.

When creating or editing a habit, the user chooses whether proof is:

- Required before the quest can be completed
- Available but optional
- Not used for that habit

Proof images are attached to individual completion records and stored through Supabase. They exist for personal accountability, not public performance.

Odyssey does not pretend that an AI can reliably judge every real-world activity from a photograph. The image is stored as evidence for the user; it is not automatically treated as unquestionable truth by a machine.

The point is accountability without pretending that one photograph can tell the whole story.

## Multiple Odysseys

People rarely have only one ambition.

A user may be preparing for an examination while building a fitness routine and completing a personal project. Odyssey allows several long-term goals to exist at once, each with its own:

- Ten-level roadmap
- Habits and tasks
- Deadlines
- Mini-bosses
- Final boss
- Progress
- Rewards

Users can move between these journeys without forcing unrelated goals into one giant progress bar.

The system should still protect focus. Multiple goals do not mean every goal must scream at the user simultaneously. The Daily Quest Board brings forward the work scheduled for today, while each roadmap preserves the wider journey behind it.

## AI-Powered Roadmaps

Large goals are usually not unclear because the user lacks ambition. They are unclear because the path contains too many invisible steps.

Odyssey uses Groq AI to make those steps visible.

### What the user provides

A useful roadmap begins with context such as:

- The goal
- The intended deadline
- Current experience or starting point
- Available days
- Available time per day
- Preferred effort level
- Limitations or constraints

### What Odyssey creates

The AI proposes:

- Ten progressive levels
- A clear purpose for every level
- Suggested recurring habits
- Suggested one-time tasks
- Milestone expectations
- Mini-boss stages at levels 3, 6, and 8
- A final-boss objective at level 10

### The user remains in control

AI helps chart the route. The user still chooses where to go and what to accept.

Before activating the roadmap, users can:

- Edit a level
- Reorder stages
- Remove an unsuitable habit
- Add their own task
- Change schedules and deadlines
- Adjust priorities and intensity
- Regenerate part or all of the plan

Nothing activates blindly. A plan generated in seconds still has to survive contact with the user's actual life.

Odyssey carries the plan beyond a generated answer. It becomes a calendar, a quest system, a measurable journey, and eventually a completed goal.

## The Ten-Level Roadmap

Every long-term goal in Odyssey becomes a ten-level journey.

Ten levels provide enough space for meaningful progression without turning the roadmap into an endless maze. Early levels establish the foundation. Middle levels demand consistency. Late levels prove mastery. Boss stages make major checkpoints visible.

| Level | Role in the journey |
| --- | --- |
| **1** | Establish the starting point and begin the first reliable actions |
| **2** | Build early consistency and prepare for the first major checkpoint |
| **3** | Face the first mini-boss |
| **4** | Strengthen the routine after the first milestone |
| **5** | Reach the middle of the journey with visible evidence of progress |
| **6** | Face the second mini-boss |
| **7** | Enter a more demanding stage of growth |
| **8** | Face the final mini-boss |
| **9** | Prepare for completion and close remaining gaps |
| **10** | Defeat the final boss and complete the Odyssey |

The structure remains consistent, but the content belongs to the goal. A fitness roadmap, examination roadmap, and creative-project roadmap may share ten levels without pretending they require the same work.

## Mini-Bosses and the Final Boss

Some milestones deserve more than a tiny green tick.

Odyssey places mini-bosses at levels 3, 6, and 8. These bosses represent meaningful checkpoints: moments where the user must demonstrate that the work from the previous levels has become real progress.

Level 10 contains the final boss—the completed form of the long-term goal.

### Boss health

Quests connected to the active stage reduce boss health when completed. More meaningful quests can contribute more than minor supporting work, allowing priority, effort, and roadmap relevance to matter while the rules remain easy to understand.

Boss health creates an immediate connection:

> Complete the work. Move the goal.

Boss progress moves only when connected work is completed. The rule stays visible: damage comes from action.

### Victory

Defeating a mini-boss can unlock:

- XP
- Rubies
- A reward chest
- A new boost
- The next roadmap stage

Defeating the final boss marks the Odyssey complete and preserves the completed journey as proof of what the user achieved.

The final screen is not the end of the user's progress. It is the moment the application stops making promises and points at the evidence.

---

## XP and Levels

XP represents permanent growth across Odyssey.

Users earn XP by completing meaningful quests, maintaining consistency, and defeating bosses. XP raises the user's overall account level and is never spent like currency.

That separation is deliberate:

- **XP answers:** How far have I grown?
- **Rubies answer:** What can I unlock or use?

Growth should not disappear because the user purchased a cosmetic reward. Once earned, XP remains part of the user's history.

Account levels exist alongside roadmap levels:

- The **account level** represents progress across every Odyssey.
- The **roadmap level** represents the current stage of one specific goal.

A user might be account level 14 while working through level 4 of a new roadmap. One records the full journey so far. The other shows the goal directly ahead.

---

## Rubies

Rubies are Odyssey's earned virtual currency.

Users receive them through real progress:

- Completing important quests
- Maintaining streaks
- Defeating mini-bosses
- Defeating final bosses
- Opening reward chests

Rubies are not purchased with real money in Odyssey. They are earned through progress. If a user has rubies, the application should be able to point toward the effort that earned them.

Rubies can unlock boosts, streak protection, chests, and cosmetic profile decorations. They make the reward system flexible without confusing currency with permanent growth.

---

## Chests, Boosts, and Cosmetic Rewards

Major rewards should feel worth stopping for.

### Reward chests

Chests can be earned through milestone victories, streak achievements, and major quest completion. They may contain:

- Rubies
- Limited-use boosts
- Streak protection
- Cosmetic profile decorations

### Boosts

Boosts provide limited benefits without completing work on the user's behalf. They can strengthen rewards, protect a streak, or support a difficult stage, but they cannot defeat a boss while the user does nothing.

The principle is simple:

> A boost can support the journey. It cannot complete the quest for the user.

### Cosmetics

Cosmetic rewards allow users to personalize their profile and display progress without affecting the fairness of the system. Decorations are trophies with personality—not power purchased through money.

Together, rubies, chests, boosts, and cosmetics create anticipation around progress while keeping the actual achievement tied to completed habits and tasks.

## Progress Analytics

Odyssey records enough information to answer a more useful question than “How many boxes did I tick?”

Users can understand progress at three levels:

### Overall progress

- Account XP and level
- Overall Odyssey streak
- Total quests completed
- Weekly and monthly consistency
- Intensity distribution
- Rubies and rewards earned
- Completed Odysseys

### Habit progress

- Current and longest streak
- Scheduled versus completed sessions
- Missed sessions
- Planned versus actual intensity
- Completion history
- Proof history where enabled

### Goal progress

- Current roadmap level
- Completed stages
- Active and defeated bosses
- Remaining boss health
- Connected quest completion
- Progress toward the final deadline

Analytics exist to help the user make better decisions. If consistency is falling, the roadmap may need adjustment. If every planned Intense session becomes Light, the schedule may be unrealistic. If low-priority quests are thriving while the final boss remains untouched, the user can see the avoidance clearly.

No judgment. No sugar-coating. Just information strong enough to be useful.

## An Example Odyssey

Imagine a user who wants to prepare for a major mathematics examination in ten weeks.

They enter:

- **Goal:** Become fully prepared for the mathematics examination
- **Deadline:** Ten weeks from today
- **Availability:** Ninety minutes on weekdays and three hours on weekends
- **Current level:** Comfortable with fundamentals but weak in calculus
- **Constraint:** School workload changes each week

Odyssey creates an editable ten-level roadmap.

### Early journey

Level 1 evaluates the syllabus and establishes a study routine. The user receives recurring habits for focused study, formula review, and short practice sessions.

Level 2 strengthens the foundation and introduces timed problem sets.

Level 3 becomes the first mini-boss: complete a mixed-topic assessment and reach the required preparation milestone.

### Middle journey

Levels 4 and 5 deepen the difficult topics. The calendar schedules High-priority calculus sessions above Low-priority organization tasks. The user records whether each study session was Light, Normal, or Intense.

Level 6 becomes the second mini-boss: complete a half-length mock examination and review every mistake.

### Final journey

Level 7 targets weak areas discovered in the mock exam.

Level 8 becomes the final mini-boss: complete a full timed paper under examination conditions.

Level 9 closes remaining gaps and shifts toward revision.

Level 10 holds the final boss: demonstrate complete readiness through the final preparation plan.

Every day, the Daily Quest Board shows the work due now. Completed sessions earn XP and rubies, maintain streaks, and reduce the active boss's health. A required photo can capture written practice when accountability matters. Missed sessions remain honest without deleting the work already completed.

The examination is still difficult. The path is now clear enough to follow.

The experience should feel like beginning a long journey from a quiet shore—adventurous without becoming chaotic, playful without becoming childish, and peaceful enough that the user's goals remain the center of attention.

## Technology

Odyssey uses a mobile-first stack that keeps development fast without sacrificing a path toward a dependable application.

| Layer | Technology | Responsibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mobile application | React Native | Native mobile experience across supported platforms |
| Development platform | Expo SDK 57 | Application tooling, device capabilities, and development workflow |
| Language | TypeScript | Safer, clearer application code |
| Backend platform | Supabase | Authentication, structured data, access control, and backend services |
| Image storage | Supabase Storage | Private storage for user-submitted proof images |
| AI provider | Groq | Generation of editable ten-level goal roadmaps |
| Reminders | Device notifications and in-app reminders | Scheduled quest and deadline notifications |

### Why Supabase?

Odyssey needs more than a place to store task titles. It needs authenticated users, connected goals, recurring quests, completion history, reward progress, and private proof images.

Supabase provides a practical foundation for those needs while keeping the data model understandable and the mobile application focused on the experience.

### Why Groq?

The AI interaction must feel immediate enough to keep the user inside the act of planning. Groq provides fast model access for turning a large goal into a structured starting point.

The model does not own the plan. It proposes one. The user reviews it, and Odyssey turns the accepted version into something actionable.

## Product Architecture

Odyssey follows a straightforward responsibility split.

1. The React Native application presents goals, calendars, quests, roadmaps, rewards, and analytics.
2. Supabase Auth identifies the user.
3. Supabase stores goals, roadmap stages, habits, tasks, completions, streaks, XP, rubies, chests, boosts, and cosmetic unlocks.
4. Supabase Storage holds private photo-proof images linked to completion records.
5. A protected server-side function communicates with Groq to generate roadmap proposals.
6. The application schedules device notifications and maintains in-app reminder states.

Groq credentials must never be embedded in the mobile application. The client requests a roadmap through a protected backend boundary, and only that server-side boundary communicates with the AI provider.

Simple where it should be simple. Protected where it must be protected.

---

## Privacy and Trust

Odyssey asks users to record personal goals, routines, progress, and sometimes photographs. That deserves more respect than a decorative privacy paragraph.

The V1 trust model follows a few non-negotiable principles:

- A user's goals, quests, completions, and rewards belong to that user.
- Proof images remain private and associated with their owner's completion records.
- Access controls must prevent one user from reading another user's data.
- Groq API credentials remain server-side.
- AI-generated plans remain suggestions until the user accepts them.
- Odyssey does not claim that stored photo proof has been automatically verified.
- Sensitive credentials and real personal data never belong in source code, fixtures, logs, or documentation.

Gamification can be playful. Trust cannot.

## The Point

Odyssey does not exist because people need another place to write down tasks.

People already have notes applications, calendars, alarms, spreadsheets, and plenty of productivity tools they stopped using.

Odyssey exists because progress deserves to feel connected.

The habit completed this morning should strengthen the streak. The streak should reveal consistency. The consistency should move the roadmap. The roadmap should weaken the boss. The boss should stand between the user and a goal they genuinely care about.

Every part points in the same direction.

That is the difference.

The user does not open Odyssey to maintain an app. They open it to continue the journey.

The quests are waiting.

The roadmap is clear.

The final boss is still standing.

That is fine. We know what the next quest is.
